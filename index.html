Malnutrition, Income, and Food Price Analysis

This project examines child malnutrition trends across Indian states using NFHS-5 data, income levels, and food price variations. Below are key findings from the study.

Key Findings

  • States with low per capita income show a higher prevalence of child malnutrition.
  • Regions where staple food prices are higher tend to have more undernourished children.
  • Government schemes in some states have helped improve child nutrition despite economic challenges.
